Here's an overview of our project.

This project aims to investigate the level of engagement that tweets spreading misinformation or disinformation about the alleged sale of Scarborough Shoal by Noynoy Aquino and Trillanes generate, compared to other factual tweets on the same topic.

Motivation

The motivation for this project is the increasing prevalence of misinformation/disinformation on social media platforms such as Twitter and its potential negative impact on public discourse and decision-making. By studying the engagement patterns of false tweets compared to factual tweets on a specific topic, this project can shed light on how misinformation/disinformation spreads on social media and how it can be effectively addressed through fact-checking and other interventions.

Problem

The problem in question for this project is to investigate the level of engagement that tweets spreading misinformation or disinformation regarding the story about Noynoy Aquino and Trillanes selling Scarborough Shoal receive on Twitter compared to factual tweets on the same topic. The project aims to explore the different types of engagements, such as likes, replies, retweets, and quote retweets, and how they change over time, with a focus on important events such as news coverage and the release of fact-checking articles.

Solution

The ultimate goal is to understand how misinformation and disinformation spread on social media and how it affects user engagement. This information can be helpful for social media companies, policymakers, and other stakeholders who are concerned about the impact of misinformation/disinformation on public opinion and democratic processes.

Here is our project formulation.

Research Question

How much engagement does a tweet spread misinformation/disinformation regarding the story about Noynoy Aquino and Trillanes selling Scarborough Shoal garner compared to other factual tweets on the same topic? How did this change over time?

Hypothesis 1

Tweets spreading misinformation/disinformation regarding the story about Noynoy Aquino and Trillanes selling Scarborough Shoal are likely to have more quote retweets and replies than likes and retweets.

Hypothesis 2

On average, tweets spreading misinformation/disinformation also have fewer engagements than factual tweets on the same topic.

Hypothesis 3

The number of engagements (i.e., likes, replies, retweets, and quote retweets) on tweets spreading misinformation/disinformation also dropped over time after the release of fact-checking articles.

Null Hypothesis

There is no notable engagement trend for tweets spreading misinformation/disinformation regarding the story about Noynoy Aquino and Trillanes selling Scarborough Shoal. There is no difference in engagement compared to factual tweets on the same topic. The number of engagements on tweets spreading misinformation/disinformation remained the same over time.

Action Plan

Analyze both factual and false tweets and compare their engagements over time and see how they changed while also taking note of important events such as news coverage and the release of fact-checking articles.
